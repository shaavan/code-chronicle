# Log 354: Choosing Where Truth Lives

> 31 March 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the currency conversion work.
- Reworked the fiat-denominated BOLT12 offer flow to use payee-side amount
  resolution.
- Updated validation to occur at the ChannelManager acceptance boundary.
- Added regression tests for mismatched fiat invoice handling.
- Cleaned up and rewrote branch history to reflect the updated design.

## Reflection

Today was about settling a design question properly.

I reworked the fiat-denominated offer flow so that amount resolution happens on
the payee side, instead of pre-filling invoice requests on the payer side. This
brings the model closer to how it naturally fits: the payee defines the final
invoice, and the payer validates it before execution.

That change also clarified where validation belongs. Instead of keeping it
buried inside payment handling, it now sits at the acceptance boundary in
ChannelManager, where both manual and automatic paths can enforce it
consistently.

Once the model felt right, I reshaped the branch to reflect it cleanly. The
history now reads as if this approach was the design from the start, without
leaving behind intermediate experiments.

I also added regression tests around mismatched fiat invoices to make the
boundary explicit and verifiable.

By the end, the flow feels simpler, more coherent, and easier to reason about.

A good day of choosing the right structure, and committing to it.
