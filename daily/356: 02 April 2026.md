# Log 356: Holding the Boundaries

> 02 April 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the currency conversion work.
- Reworked `CurrencyConversion` ownership to remain with `ChannelManager`.
- Updated `OffersMessageFlow` to take conversion explicitly at call sites.
- Cleaned up PR history into clear, atomic commits.
- Added targeted test coverage across currency conversion and BOLT12 flows.
- Fixed outbound payment correctness issues around invoice replay and async
  failure handling.
- Reviewed and corrected outdated invariants in `outbound_payment.rs`.

## Reflection

Today was about tightening boundaries and making the system behave cleanly
around them.

I reworked the currency-conversion stack so that `CurrencyConversion` stays
owned by `ChannelManager`, while the offer flow takes it explicitly where
needed. This makes the flow easier to follow and avoids hiding important
dependencies inside state.

From there, I spent time making the branch itself easier to reason about.
Commits now reflect single logical changes, placed where they belong, instead of
being layered on top.

A good part of the day also went into strengthening correctness. I added tests
around currency flows, parsing behavior, and edge cases like replayed invoices
and async failures. While doing that, I found and fixed a couple of issues where
payments could behave incorrectly or get stuck.

I also revisited some old assumptions. One debug/assert path still reflected a
“guaranteed at parse time” invariant that no longer holds after the fiat
changes. Catching that felt important.

By the end, things feel tighter. Boundaries are clearer, failure paths are more
explicit, and the system is easier to trust.

A solid day of making the edges hold.
