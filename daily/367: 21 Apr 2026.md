# Log 367: Sitting and Looking

> 21 April 2026

## What I worked on

- Wrote my report for the past quarter and went back through the work closely
  enough to account for it without leaning on memory or momentum.
- Reconstructed the main threads of the quarter so I could see which efforts
  had actually compounded and which only felt large while I was inside them.
- Drafted a proposal for the next year around the kinds of problems I want to
  stay close to and the work I now feel ready to commit to more seriously.
- Used both documents together to test whether the direction I want to claim
  for the next year is supported by the work I have already done.

## Reflection

Today moved away from implementation, but it did not feel separate from the
work.

Writing the quarter report made me sit with the last few months more carefully
than I had while moving through them. It is easy to carry a loose sense of
progress from one week to the next and let that stand in for a real account.
Going back through the quarter in detail made that harder in a useful way. Some
work that had felt scattered in the middle looked much more coherent once I had
to lay it out plainly. The continuity was stronger than I had been carrying day
to day.

The proposal for next year pushed from the other side. Instead of explaining
what had happened, I had to say what I want to keep building toward, which
problems still feel worth staying close to, and what now seems solid enough to
commit to more openly. Putting that beside the quarter report made the distance
between them useful. One had to show the ground already covered. The other had
to say where I mean to keep going.

Seeing those two documents next to each other changed the day. The proposal did
not feel separate from the quarter that came before it. It felt like the same
line, just read in the other direction. By the end, the way forward felt less
like something I needed to manufacture and more like something already present
in the work, if I was willing to sit still long enough to see it.
