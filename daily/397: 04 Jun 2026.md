# Log 397: What the Spec Was Carrying

> 04 June 2026

## What I worked on

- Finished writing up the recurrence-spec design update after several days of
  reworking the model and checking which parts of the implementation pressure
  were really coming from the document underneath it.
- Named the concrete failures in the current recurrence spec instead of
  leaving them as a general sense of awkwardness, including places where the
  design stays too hard to reason about, too easy to implement unreliably, and
  too willing to carry a privacy risk across repeated payments.
- Consolidated the replacement ideas into one coherent recurrence design so
  the fixes no longer read like scattered local corrections.
- Prepared the design in a form I can now take back to other contributors for
  criticism instead of still treating it as private implementation cleanup.

## Reflection

This day felt quieter than the earlier recurrence work, but more decisive.

The hard part was that I was no longer trying to invent better ideas from
scratch. I was trying to look back over the current spec and over my own notes
from the last few days closely enough to say exactly what was wrong, why it
was wrong, and whether the replacement really held together as one design. Up
to this point, a lot of the pressure had shown up as resistance during
implementation. The useful change today was that the resistance became
legible. I could point to the places where the current recurrence shape asks
too much interpretation from the implementer, where that looseness would make
behavior drift across implementations, and where the identity model was still
keeping more continuity than it should.

What I liked by the end was that the answer stopped looking like a pile of
repairs. The new ideas finally read as one thing. That matters partly because
it makes the next implementation pass less confused, but mostly because it
means I can now put the design in front of other contributors without feeling
like the code is compensating for a spec I still do not trust.
