# Log 361: Letting It Wait

> 10 April 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the currency conversion work.
- Replayed the invoice-request builder changes on top of the new `MsatsRange`
  model.
- Reworked BOLT12 request and invoice quoting so omitted fiat amounts stay
  omitted on the wire and are resolved later by the payee.
- Replaced exact payer-side fiat amount checks with range-based validation
  against the expected payable amount.
- Reviewed the next round of feedback and isolated the remaining TOCTOU risk in
  omitted-fiat request construction.

## Reflection

Today was about letting the flow wait until it actually knows enough.

The earlier shape still pulled fiat conversion too far forward. It worked, but
it forced the request-building path to commit to values before the protocol had
reached the point where those values were really meant to exist. I spent the
day replaying that stack on top of `MsatsRange` and cleaning up the semantics
so the model matches the intended BOLT12 flow more closely.

That shift clarified several things at once. Omitted request amounts now stay
omitted on the wire, payees resolve fiat offer amounts when they construct the
invoice, and payer-side validation no longer depends on brittle exact equality
between independently resolved values. Moving that check to range containment
makes the behavior feel less like a workaround and more like the right contract.

The review pass also helped separate what is already fixed from what is merely
less visible. The old invoice-amount bug is no longer the real issue. The live
pressure now sits earlier, in the redundant omitted-fiat conversion that still
happens during request construction before
`check_amount_msats_for_quantity(...)`.

So the day ended with a cleaner model and a narrower problem. The remaining
question is not whether fiat support works, but whether request signing should
be doing that conversion work at all, or whether the design becomes safer once
that responsibility is deferred to the later invoice and payment stages where
the amount is actually meant to be verified.
