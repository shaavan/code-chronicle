## What I worked on

- Reworked the stack around a canonical `MsatsRange` abstraction.
- Split and replayed the early PR commits so `CurrencyConversion` and range

## Reflection

Today was about finding the right shape for the amount model.

The earlier version worked in pieces, but it still felt like range-aware logic
was being introduced sideways. So I stepped back and rebuilt the stack around a
more central abstraction: `MsatsRange`.

That changed the order of the work in an important way. I split the
`CurrencyConversion` introduction from the range-resolution work, then replayed
the rest of the stack on top of that cleaner base. Once the pieces landed in
the right order, the flow became easier to read and easier to defend.

From there, the plumbing started to line up more naturally. `ChannelManager`
now carries the conversion dependency explicitly, offer amount resolution now
speaks in ranges instead of raw values, and `MsatsRange` itself now feels like a
real part of the model rather than a helper hidden at the edges.

The tests helped confirm that this was more than a refactor. By tightening
coverage around unsupported and invalid conversions, the boundaries became more
explicit too.

By the end, the work felt more coherent. The stack now has a clearer center,
and the rest of the conversion flow has something solid to build on.
