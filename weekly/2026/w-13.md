# Weekly Log 13: Returning, Refining, Settling

> 26 March 2026 – 28 March 2026

## Overview

This was a short but meaningful stretch of work.

The focus was on returning to momentum after a brief slowdown, closing out the
dummy hop follow-up updates, and then spending time bringing the currency
conversion work into a cleaner, more coherent shape—both in code and in history.

## Highlights

### Dummy Hops: Closing the Loop

- Resumed work after recovery and addressed the remaining suggestions on the
  dummy hop follow-up branch.  
  ([Branch][DummyFollow])

- Updated the PR with changes that tightened internal consistency, especially
  around how dummy-hop fees are handled as local receive-side state.

- Brought the work to a stable and defensible state, aligning surrounding
  mechanics such as minimum HTLC handling and preserving compatibility with
  existing formats.

### Currency Conversion: Making It Coherent

- Revisited the fiat-offer parsing and invoice-building flow to rebuild a clear
  understanding of how the pieces fit together.

- Cleaned up the commit history to make it easier to follow:

  - Split mixed commits into atomic units
  - Folded follow-up fixes into their logical places
  - Rewrote commit messages to align with AGENTS.md guidelines

- Improved readability in the fiat invoice serialization logic through better
  naming and focused comments.

- Revisited the tolerance work, keeping the `(f64, u8)` API shape while backing
  out its usage from the rest of the flow to keep the current design simpler and
  easier to reason about.  
  ([Branch][CurrencyBranch])

## Reflections

This stretch was quieter, but important.

After a slower start due to recovery, the work gradually picked up rhythm again.
The dummy hop follow-up reached a point where the changes felt internally
consistent and easier to stand behind.

The currency work, on the other hand, was about bringing coherence. Not just
making the code work, but making it readable, traceable, and aligned with the
way it should be understood by others.

One thing that stood out is how much clarity comes from cleaning up history.
When commits reflect intent clearly, the code itself becomes easier to reason
about.

Nothing flashy this week. But things feel more settled, more deliberate, and
better shaped for what comes next.
