# Weekly Log 11: Framing One Thread, Finishing Another

> 12 March 2026 – 16 March 2026

## Overview

This week was split across two distinct efforts.

On one side, I moved the **recurrence** work forward by extracting its core
design questions from the PoC and opening them up for discussion. On the other,
I brought the **dummy hop follow-up** work through its final stages: extending
the implementation, getting the tests right, introducing correctness checks, and
polishing the branch into a stable, review-ready state.

## Highlights

### Recurrence: From Implementation to Discussion

- Revisited the recurrence PoC to rebuild a clear understanding of the full flow
  after some time away from the code.
- Identified that while the implementation demonstrates the mechanism, the
  underlying design decisions are implicit and scattered across the code.
- Extracted the key decision points and structured them into a focused design
  discussion.  
  ([Discussion][RecurrenceDiscussion])
- Opened the issue to enable deliberate, well-scoped discussion around the
  recurrence design before moving toward implementation.

### Dummy Hops: Completing the Follow-up Work

- Continued the dummy hop follow-up across multiple iterations, focusing on
  correctness and completeness.  
  ([Branch][DummyFollow])

- Extended skimmed fee handling by propagating the field to `PaymentClaimed`,
  ensuring the information remains available at the end of the payment
  lifecycle.

- Implemented and finalized tests for the skimmed fee behavior, resolving issues
  through multiple iterations until the flow became stable.

- Introduced underpayment checks to ensure dummy hops behave like real hops,
  failing payments that do not satisfy fee and CLTV requirements.

- Reviewed and polished the branch with the help of [CodeRabbit], uncovering and
  fixing several important bugs along with smaller issues.

- Brought the branch to a stable, review-ready state.

## Reflections

This week carried two different modes of work: **framing** and **finishing**.

The recurrence work required stepping back. The implementation already existed,
but the real task was to make the underlying decisions visible. Extracting those
questions and placing them into discussion felt like the necessary first step
toward a well-reasoned design.

The dummy hop work moved in the opposite direction. It was about closing the
loop: extending the implementation, validating it through tests, enforcing
correctness through underpayment checks, and finally polishing the branch until
it felt stable.

One pattern that stood out is how different these phases demand different
thinking. Framing requires distance and clarity; finishing requires attention to
detail and persistence through small, often hidden issues.

By the end of the week, both threads moved forward in meaningful ways.
Recurrence now has a clear place for discussion, and dummy hops have reached a
point where the work feels complete from my side and ready for review.

A good week of shaping and closure.
