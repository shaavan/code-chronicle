# Weekly Log 14: Reset, Repair, Refine

> 31 March 2026 – 07 April 2026

## Overview

This week focused on reshaping the currency-conversion work at a foundational
level.

The work moved through a sequence of steps: settling the core design around
payee-side amount resolution, fixing regressions and restoring guarantees,
tightening ownership and validation boundaries, rebuilding the stack on clearer
amount semantics, and identifying the need for a more principled approach to
handling fiat conversion drift.

## Highlights

### Payee-Side Resolution: Choosing the Model

- Reworked the fiat-denominated BOLT12 offer flow to resolve amounts on the
  payee side instead of pre-filling invoice requests on the payer side.  
  ([Branch][PayeeResolution])

- Moved validation to the acceptance boundary in `ChannelManager`, ensuring both
  manual and automatic paths enforce the same checks.

- Added regression tests and reshaped the branch history to reflect this model
  cleanly.

### Correctness: Restoring the Guarantees

- Identified and fixed regressions in amount validation and parsing behavior.

- Restored strict validation for bitcoin-denominated offers while preserving
  relaxed behavior for fiat flows.

- Corrected a persistence compatibility issue and documented constraints in
  AGENTS.md to prevent similar mistakes.

### Boundaries: Ownership and Failure Paths

- Reworked the currency-conversion stack so `CurrencyConversion` remains owned
  by `ChannelManager`, with explicit threading through call sites.

- Strengthened failure handling in outbound payments, including replayed invoice
  rejection and async resolution failure cleanup.

- Added targeted test coverage across currency flows and edge cases.  
  ([Branch][Currency87])

### Foundations: Clarifying Amount Semantics

- Split `InvoiceRequest` amount semantics into explicit request amounts and
  effective payable amounts.

- Replayed and cleaned the currency-conversion stack on top of this clearer
  foundation.

- Simplified public APIs to align behavior with primary methods rather than
  wrapper variants.  
  ([Branch][Currency91])

### Edges: Handling Fiat Drift

- Replayed invoice-quoting logic to align with the new model and APIs.

- Identified fiat conversion drift as a result of exact comparison of
  independently resolved amounts.

- Concluded that a central tolerance or range-based helper is needed to replace
  ad hoc validation logic.  
  ([Branch][Currency92])

## Reflections

This week felt like working from the inside out.

It started with choosing the right model, then moved through fixing what broke,
clarifying ownership, and rebuilding the stack on cleaner semantics. Along the
way, several hidden assumptions surfaced, especially around validation and
amount handling.

One thing that stood out is how often correctness issues come from unclear
boundaries. Once ownership and responsibility are placed clearly, many of those
issues become easier to reason about.

Another is that rebuilding on a clean foundation pays off quickly. Once the
amount semantics were clarified, the rest of the work began to align more
naturally.

The week ended with a new question rather than a finished piece: how to handle
fiat conversion drift properly. The current fixes work, but they point toward a
missing abstraction that needs to be introduced.

A strong week of resetting direction, restoring guarantees, and refining the
structure for what comes next.
