# Log 357: Seeing the Whole

> 03 April 2026

## What I worked on

- [Updated] the currency conversion PR with validated fixes from AI-generated
  findings.
- Cleaned up commit structure, messages, and overall PR presentation.
- Developed a review approach based on invariants and data flow; analyzed PR
  scope and evolution.
- Rebased onto upstream, resolved drift, and verified with a full test pass.

## Reflection

Today was about stepping back and seeing the PR as a whole.

I went through a set of AI-generated findings, applying only the ones that held
up under scrutiny. Some suggestions were useful, others weren’t. Filtering that
signal felt like an important part of the work.

From there, I spent time cleaning up the structure of the branch, both in terms
of commits and how the changes are presented. The goal was not just correctness,
but making the work easier to review and reason about.

I also paused to question whether the PR itself was becoming too large. Looking
at it more carefully, it turned out to be broad, but not unnecessarily so. That
shifted the problem from “reduce size” to “review it well.”

That led to thinking more deliberately about how to approach review. Instead of
reading linearly, it makes more sense to track invariants and data flow, and
ensure each part of the system still holds together under those lenses.

Rebasing the branch onto upstream and getting the full test suite passing was a
good final checkpoint. It confirms that the work still stands cleanly against
the current state of the codebase.

By the end, the PR felt less like a collection of changes and more like a
coherent unit.

A day of stepping back, filtering signal, and making the whole easier to see.
