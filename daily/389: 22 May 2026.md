# Log 389: Where the Price Lands

> 22 May 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the BOLT 12 currency work on the `currency-99` branch by
  splitting `InvoiceRequest` amount handling into two paths: the millisatoshi
  value explicitly named by the payer, and the amount that can still be
  derived later from the offer and quantity.
- Relaxed currency-offer request handling so a payer can ask for an invoice
  without pinning an exact msat total up front, leaving amount resolution with
  the payee side that actually owns the offer price.
- Reworked invoice construction to derive currency-priced totals through the
  conversion interface when needed instead of assuming the request always
  carries a payable amount already, and chose the lower end of the accepted
  range when conversion returns bounds.
- Tightened the surrounding invoice-path coverage so these currency flows are
  exercised through real request and invoice creation behavior rather than only
  through local helpers.

## Reflection

Today was mostly about ownership.

The amount model had started to make room for currency-denominated offers, but
`InvoiceRequest` was still behaving like every serious payment flow begins with
the payer already holding one fixed millisatoshi answer. That did not fit the
feature anymore. If the offer is priced in currency, the payer can describe
intent without being the one that resolves the final bitcoin amount. Splitting
the explicit request amount from the later derived amount made that visible in
the code instead of leaving it buried in helper behavior.

That pushed invoice creation into a much better position. The payee side could
stop pretending it was merely echoing back a number the request had already
settled and instead do the real work of turning the offer into a payable
invoice. Choosing the lower end of the accepted conversion range mattered for
the same reason. Once the payee owns resolution, it should not also get to use
ambiguity as room to quote upward.

By the end of the day the flow felt less confused about who is allowed to know
what, and when. The payer can leave the msat amount open for a currency-priced
offer, and the payee can resolve it honestly at invoice time. That made the
feature read more like one protocol exchange and less like currency support had
been threaded into a bitcoin-first path by exception.
