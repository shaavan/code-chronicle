# Log 404: One Amount Through

> 15 June 2026

## What I worked on

- Continued the BOLT 12 currency work on the `currency-110` branch by
  tightening invoice construction so a currency-priced offer resolves to one
  concrete millisatoshi amount before the response paths branch apart.
- Reworked both invoice-response paths to carry a pre-resolved
  `amount_msats` through builder creation instead of recomputing currency
  conversion later inside `respond_with` and
  `respond_using_derived_keys`.
- Kept that resolved amount aligned with the rest of the receive flow, so
  inbound payment registration and blinded payment path selection now use the
  same number the issued invoice is built around.
- Preserved the public response APIs while pushing the commitment to one
  converted amount earlier in the internal flow.

## Reflection

This came after the heavier currency validation work, when most of the flow was
already present and the remaining problems were easier to see as consistency
problems instead of missing features.

The awkward part was that invoice construction could still arrive at the same
converted amount more than once, in slightly different places, while other
parts of the receive path had already started depending on that number. That
left too much resting on the assumption that repeated conversions would keep
landing on the same answer. The code still worked, but it was asking separate
pieces of the path to stay in sync by luck and discipline.

What mattered today was making the response flow choose that amount once and
carry it forward from there. Once the builder paths took a resolved
`amount_msats`, the rest of the logic read more plainly. The invoice amount,
the inbound payment setup, and the blinded path selection were no longer
parallel guesses at the same thing. They were all following one earlier
decision, which made the whole path feel less delicate.
