## Reflection

Today was a day of cleaning things up until they made sense.

I started by walking through the fiat-offer flow again, tracing how parsing,
invoice requests, and invoice construction fit together. That helped clarify why
certain pieces existed, especially around msat validation and how resolved
amounts are carried through serialization and parsing.

With that clarity in place, I turned to the commit history. It had grown in a
way that worked, but didn’t read cleanly. So I spent time reshaping it:
splitting mixed commits, folding small fixes into their rightful places, and
aligning the messages with the AGENTS.md guidelines.

That process carried into the code as well. Some parts became easier to read
just by naming things better and tightening the comments, without adding new
layers.

I also revisited the tolerance work. The API shape felt right, so I kept it, but
backed out its usage from the rest of the flow. The result is simpler for now,
and easier to build on later without confusion.

By the end, both the code and the history felt more coherent. Not just working,
but easier to follow and reason about.

A quiet but satisfying kind of progress.
