# Log 420: Before the Amount Travels

> 08 July 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the currency-payment branch by closing underpayment paths on
  both sides of a currency-denominated invoice request.
- Validated explicit recipient-side amounts against the converted,
  quantity-scaled minimum before async keysend claims can proceed.
- Made `ChannelManager::pay_for_offer` reject insufficient or unrepresentable
  explicit amounts before signing and sending the request.
- Documented `CurrencyConversion` as a synchronous, cached-data callback that
  must avoid I/O and re-entry into LDK while internal locks may be held.
- Added downgrade guidance for currency invoice requests left in pending
  outbound-payment state when returning to LDK 0.2.

## Reflection

The currency work already had a deliberate gap: generic invoice-request
parsing and construction cannot validate a converted amount because those
layers do not own a converter. That separation still holds, but two callers
had treated the deferred check as if it had already happened.

On the recipient side, `payable_amount_msats` accepted an explicit payer
amount after the request had been authenticated. A one-msat request could
therefore reach the async keysend claim path even when the converted offer
required more. The payer side had the same gap earlier. `pay_for_offer`
confirmed that conversion succeeded, discarded the returned range, then could
sign an amount below its own converted minimum.

Closing both paths made the placement of the checks more concrete. The
recipient validates when it has the converter and quantity needed to decide
what can be claimed. The payer validates before it signs a request the
recipient must reject. Generic builders remain independent of conversion
policy, while the entry points that can act on an amount are responsible for
checking it. Focused tests now cover both underpayment paths.

Tracing those calls also exposed what implementing `CurrencyConversion`
requires at runtime. Conversion may happen while `ChannelManager` and outbound
payment locks are held, so fetching a rate over I/O or calling back into LDK
can block the payment path or deadlock it. The trait now says directly that
implementations should return cached data synchronously and avoid re-entry.
That constraint was already present in the call graph; documenting it makes it
part of the API rather than something users discover from a lockup.

The downgrade warning belongs to the same boundary work. LDK 0.2 cannot read a
persisted currency invoice request in pending outbound-payment state, and one
such request can prevent the entire `ChannelManager` from deserializing. The
changelog now tells users to clear those payments before persisting state for
a downgrade. Today left fewer implied contracts around currency amounts: each
side checks before the amount travels further, and the operational limits of
conversion and persistence are written where callers can see them.
