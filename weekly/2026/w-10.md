# Weekly Log 10: Closing One Thread, Opening Another

> 04 March 2026 – 10 March 2026

## Overview

This week marked the final stretch of the **currency conversion** work: fixing
the last CI issues, preparing the branch for review, addressing follow-up
feedback, and getting it into a clean, review-ready shape. At the same time,
with that thread nearing closure, I began turning back toward **recurrence** —
this time with a clearer intent to surface its key design decisions in a form
that can be discussed and reasoned about more openly.

## Highlights

### Currency Conversion: From Final Fixes to Review Refinement

- Made solid progress on the remaining CI failures for the currency branch,
  especially around the `c_bindings` side of the implementation.
- Tightened the commit-by-commit narrative of the branch, making the “what” and
  “why” of each change clearer before review.
- Finished the last round of CI fixes and brought the branch to a complete,
  presentable state.
- Rebased the branch on `main`, opened it up for review, and moved the work from
  implementation into discussion and refinement.  
  ([Branch update][Currency66])
- Addressed subtle follow-up concerns raised during review, including a few edge
  cases surfaced with help from [CodeRabbit][CodeRabbit].
- By the end of the week, the branch felt significantly tighter and better
  aligned with reviewer expectations.  
  ([Latest progress][Currency69])

### Returning to Recurrence

- Revisited the recurrence work after letting it sit for some time.
- Reflected on the complete draft implementation and identified that several
  decisions made along the way deserve deeper reasoning and peer discussion.  
  ([Draft approach][RecurrenceDraft])
- Had a productive discussion with my mentor on how to finish recurrence
  step-by-step, breaking the remaining work into clearer, discussable pieces.
- Spent time mapping out the major decision points in recurrence so they can be
  brought into coherent discussion with colleagues.

## Reflections

This week was less about building something new and more about carrying one
thread across the finish line with care.

The currency work moved through an important final phase: not just becoming
complete, but becoming readable, reviewable, and resilient under feedback. That
kind of finish matters. A branch is not done when the code merely works — it is
done when the reasoning can be followed and the design can stand in front of
others clearly.

At the same time, returning to recurrence felt timely. With currency and dummy
hops now in a much stronger place, I have more space to revisit recurrence with
the patience it deserves. The implementation draft already exists; now the task
is to make its decision points visible enough that others can reason through
them too.

A good week of closure, refinement, and setting the next chapter in motion.
