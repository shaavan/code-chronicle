# Log 355: Restoring the Guarantees

> 01 April 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the currency conversion work.
- Identified and fixed regressions in Bolt12 amount-validation logic.
- Simplified payer-side invoice amount checks in `channelmanager.rs`.
- Corrected a persistence compatibility issue in `PendingOutboundPayment`.
- Fixed parse-time validation behavior in `invoice.rs` for bitcoin-denominated
  offers.
- Restored regression tests for omitted-amount validation.

## Reflection

Today was about finding and fixing what quietly broke.

While working through the Bolt12 amount-validation changes, I came across a few
regressions that affected correctness in subtle ways.

One part was simplifying the payer-side validation so it checks against the
original invoice request at the right place, while still preserving the expected
error behavior.

Another was a persistence issue, where a TLV field had been reused with a
different meaning. That kind of change is unsafe, so I corrected the layout and
documented the rule to avoid this class of problems going forward.

The most important fix was in parsing. Bitcoin-denominated offers with omitted
amounts had lost their strict validation, which meant mismatched invoice amounts
could slip through. I restored that behavior so deterministic amounts are still
enforced during parsing, while keeping fiat flows intentionally relaxed.

By the end, the system felt back in a safer state. The guarantees that should
hold are holding again.

A good day of catching what matters.
