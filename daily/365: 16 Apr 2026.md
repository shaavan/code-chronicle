# Log 365: The Surface Holds

> 16 April 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the recurrence transition work on the `rust-recurrence` branch.
- Reintroduced recurrence-capable primitives across `Offer`,
  `InvoiceRequest`, and `Invoice` on top of the fresh reset branch.
- Added semantic-validation coverage around that restored protocol surface so
  the new objects were checked as behavior, not just reinstated as types.
- Refactored invoice creation around explicit `created_at` so basetime can be
  owned outside `rust-lightning`.
- Added explicit payer-signing-pubkey support to invoice-request construction
  as a generic signer seam for higher layers.

## Reflection

Today was the first day the reset branch started to feel concrete.

Restarting from `upstream/main` had clarified the recurrence plan, but that
only goes so far until the new branch begins to carry real weight again. The
work today happened low in the protocol surface: rebuilding the
recurrence-capable pieces in `Offer`, `InvoiceRequest`, and `Invoice`, then
making sure the validation logic around them still held once those pieces were
back in place.

That mattered because these pieces are what remain once the old PoC-specific
session machinery is stripped away. Reintroducing them on the clean branch made
the distinction sharper. This was no longer about preserving old work out of
habit. It was about identifying which parts still belong once recurrence is no
longer being driven from inside `ChannelManager`.

The builder-level seam work mattered too. Moving invoice creation to an
explicit `created_at` and letting invoice-request construction take an
explicit payer signing pubkey both pointed in the same direction:
`rust-lightning` should expose the protocol material cleanly enough that a
higher layer can own timing and signing decisions without special-case
recurrence hooks leaking back downward.

The reset stopped being only an architectural claim. The branch was starting
to show, in actual objects and builders, what would stay in `rust-lightning`
once the recurrence-specific control flow moved out.
