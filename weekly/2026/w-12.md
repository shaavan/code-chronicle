## Reflections

This week highlighted how different kinds of progress coexist.

The dummy hop work moved through a full cycle: from final fixes to review-ready
state and PR submission. It was a reminder that finishing well matters just as
much as building.

The currency work, on the other hand, stayed in iteration. It required staying
with the problem, incorporating feedback, and refining the design rather than
rushing toward closure.

Recurrence was at an earlier stage, where progress came not through code but
through clarity. Engaging with the discussion and helping shape the next steps
felt like a different but equally important form of contribution.

A pattern that stood out this week is that not all work moves at the same speed.
Some threads close, some evolve, and some are still being defined.

Learning to move between these modes without forcing premature closure feels
like an important part of the process.

This week felt less like completing tasks and more like managing the state of
multiple threads at once.

A strong week of completion, refinement, and direction.
