# Log 390: The Price Has Edges

> 23 May 2026

## What I worked on

- [Continued] the BOLT 12 currency work on the `currency-99` branch by adding
  payer-side invoice verification for currency-denominated offers so returned
  invoices are checked against the originating offer instead of being trusted
  as quoted.
- Used the upper end of the accepted conversion range when verifying those
  invoices, pairing it with yesterday's lower-bound invoice construction so the
  full offer-to-payment path now stays inside an explicit band.
- Split invoice rejection more precisely between invoices whose amounts cannot
  be verified at all and invoices whose amounts can be checked but still come
  back too high.
- Carried the same amount-derivation and validation rules through adjacent
  offer-payment paths, then added end-to-end coverage for currency-priced
  offers that request an invoice, receive a converted amount, verify it, and
  complete payment.

## Reflection

Yesterday put price resolution in the payee's hands. Today was about making
sure that did not quietly turn into trust.

Once the payee could turn a currency-priced offer into a real invoice, the
missing question was what the payer still gets to verify on the other end. The
answer ended up being narrower and better than I expected. The payer does not
need to recompute the payee's exact number. It only needs to know whether that
number still lands inside the range the offer allows. Using the upper bound at
verification completed the shape of the exchange. The payee resolves
uncertainty downward when issuing the invoice, and the payer resolves it upward
when deciding whether the invoice is still safe to pay.

That also made the failure cases feel more honest. There is a real difference
between an invoice that cannot be checked because the conversion basis is
missing and an invoice that can be checked and still comes back too high.
Splitting those outcomes pulled the tolerance model out of helper behavior and
made it visible in the protocol path itself.

By the end of the day the feature finally held together as one bounded payment
flow instead of a few compatible pieces. The offer can be priced in currency,
the payee can quote inside the allowed floor, the payer can verify against the
ceiling, and the payment path now behaves like those boundaries were designed
on purpose from the start.
