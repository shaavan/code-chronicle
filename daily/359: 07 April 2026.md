## What I worked on

- [Continued] the currency conversion work.

- Identified and analyzed the fiat conversion drift issue and its root cause.

## Reflection

Today was about getting the details right at the edges.

I replayed the invoice-quoting portion of the work, making sure the behavior
stays consistent: omitted amounts remain omitted on the wire, payees resolve
fiat offers into invoice amounts, and explicit payer amounts still enforce the
minimum implied by the offer.

On the payer side, the validation now fits more cleanly with the refactored
amount APIs, which makes the flow easier to follow.

While revisiting the drift fix, it became clear that the core issue wasn’t the
specific check, but the assumption behind it. Comparing independently resolved
fiat amounts exactly is too strict and leads to mismatches.

The current workaround works, but it doesn’t feel right. A better direction is
to introduce a central tolerance or range-based helper that can be used
consistently across the codebase.

That feels like the next piece to get right.
